# Looker Studio Dashboard - Google Merchandise Store Data

## Project Overview

This project focuses on building an interactive **Looker Studio dashboard** using **Google Analytics data** from the Google Merchandise Store to analyse user behaviour, traffic acquisition, and marketing funnel performance.

The dashboard was designed to break down the customer journey across **TOFU (Top of Funnel), MOFU (Middle of Funnel), and BOFU (Bottom of Funnel)** stages, helping identify where users enter, engage, and drop off before conversion. Through this analysis, the project uncovered **$387K in Q1 2025 sales**, revealed a strong **47.9% dependence on direct traffic**, and showed that **72.3% of revenue was concentrated within a limited acquisition mix**. It also highlighted a **0.06% conversion gap**, pointing to opportunities for optimisation in the checkout and conversion process.

In addition, funnel segmentation helped trace the movement from **product views (100%) to cart additions (18.5%)**, while also identifying **6,497 engaged sessions with checkout abandonment**, giving clear direction for marketing and execution planning.

## Project back ground

- The Google Merchandise Store dataset provides website and user interaction data that can be used to understand how visitors discover the store, interact with products, and move through the purchase journey.

- The goal of this project was to transform raw analytics data into a dashboard that could answer practical business questions such as traffic source performance, revenue contribution, funnel movement, and user drop-off points.

- This dashboard helps translate web analytics into performance insights that are useful for marketing strategy and business decision-making.

- The project was structured to analyse the customer journey across TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU stages so that user behaviour and conversion opportunities could be understood clearly.

***

### Questions answered through the dashboard

- How much total sales were generated in Q1 2025?
- Which traffic channels contributed the most sessions and revenue?
- How dependent is the business on direct traffic?
- How concentrated is revenue across acquisition sources?
- What percentage of users move from viewing a product to adding it to cart?
- How many engaged users abandon the checkout process?
- Where does the largest funnel drop-off occur?
- What areas show potential for improving the conversion process?

***

## Dashboard design approach

- The dashboard was designed to present insights in a simple and decision-oriented format.
- The focus was on acquisition performance, revenue contribution by channel, funnel stage analysis, conversion efficiency, checkout abandonment insights, and actionable business observations.
- This structure makes it easier to evaluate both high-level business performance and detailed user behaviour in one place.

***

## Key Insights

- Generated **$387K in Q1 2025 sales**
- Found **47.9% reliance on direct traffic**, showing strong dependence on a single acquisition source
- Observed **72.3% revenue concentration**, indicating that a limited set of channels drove most of the business outcome
- Identified a **0.06% conversion gap**, highlighting room for process-level optimisation
- Funnel analysis showed a drop from **100% product views to 18.5% cart additions**
- Flagged **6,497 engaged sessions with checkout abandonment**, creating a strong case for improving the final conversion stage
